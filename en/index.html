Directions

Before navigating you through the Irish transport, I may mention that you should not be surprised if the name of a station or a bus stop seems to change. This is because, in Ireland, they use both names: the English and the Gaelic. So each stop has two names. I will mention only the English ones below. Another local custom to mention is that they say “thank you” to the bus driver every time they get off the bus. You are encouraged to do the same if you don’t want to be rude. The bus ticket can be purchased on the bus, but they accept only coins and don’t give you the change. Dart’s tickets can be purchased at any station from a machine. You can select your preferred language and pay cash or by card.

How to get to the hotel from the airport by public transport: take the bus n. 747 (with this bus, it takes 17 minutes to get to the city centre, the fee is €7 single ticket, there is a discount if you buy the return ticket at the same time). Or you can take cheaper buses such as n. 41 & n. 16 (with those busses, it takes 45 minutes to get to the city centre, the fee is €3.30). Once you get to the city centre, take the Dart at Connolly Station toward Greystones and get off at Dún Laoghaire station. The hotel is within walking distance.

How to get to the hotel from the city centre with public transport: take the Dart (train) at Connolly Station, get off at Dún Laoghaire. The hotel is within walking distance.
